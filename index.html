Understanding the problem

The World Health Organization (WHO) reported in 2018 that Canadians could expect to live up to 82.8 years, which is an increase of over 10 years since the 1960s. However, major global events such as the COVID-19 pandemic and the Ukraine War have had a significant impact on people's lives. Seniors, in particular, have been severely affected, with 33% reporting that their mental health has worsened during the pandemic, according to Statistics Canada.

Pervasive technologies have the potential to enhance the self-sufficiency, social engagement, and confidence of elderly people by enabling them to lead more active and healthy independent lives. However, the acceptance and adoption of these technologies have been limited, partly due to the lack of adaptable and elder-friendly design. To ensure that the aging population benefits from advances in pervasive computing, there needs to be a focus on designing user-friendly technologies that cater to the diversity of aging populations.

Research

Creating applications specifically for seniors is not a novel concept. At present, there are several applications and services accessible that enable senior consumers to manage their physical and mental well-being. However, most of them do not offer complete elder-friendly design or have an overwhelming number of features that can be confusing.

Surveys

Through our research, we gained a deeper understanding of the issue at hand. With this knowledge, we developed a series of questions and crafted a Google Form survey to collect responses from the general public on ways to support the physical and mental wellbeing of elderly users.

Subsequently, the survey findings were translated into charts to facilitate a more effective representation of the accumulated data.

Using the data collected from the nursing home visit, we created charts with the assistance of an online tool called Visme. The resulting data indicated that 82% of the participants surveyed were over the age of 65. Furthermore, the majority of respondents found application navigation to be the most daunting aspect due to its complexity, in addition to difficulties in reading and recalling usernames and passwords. Additionally, over 70% of the participants expressed a preference for spending their leisure time with family.

Analysis

After completing the research phase, we proceeded to the analysis stage of the design process armed with the insights we had gained. In this stage, we aimed to understand why users would prefer an easy-to-use application for socializing with family and friends.

User Personas

Based on the survey responses, we created user personas to represent potential users of our app. Each persona includes background information, their goals for using an elder-friendly video conferencing tool, and the challenges they face when using similar applications.

Visual Design

Color Palette

The choice of a color palette is an important decision when creating any design or visual material. The final color palette is a popular choice for several reasons. Green and blue are calming and soothing colors that are often associated with nature, growth, and stability. These colors work well together, creating a harmonious and balanced effect. Off-white, on the other hand, is a neutral color that provides a clean and sophisticated background, allowing the green and blue to stand out while maintaining a sense of elegance. Additionally, this color palette is versatile and can be used in various contexts, from corporate branding to website design.

Typography

Montserrat has a clean and straightforward design that makes it easy to read even at smaller font sizes. It is a sans-serif typeface that is popular for both digital and print media due to its versatility and legibility. Additionally, Montserrat's modern look makes it a perfect choice for branding, headlines, and body text.

Final Design

Startup - First launch

To simplify the user experience, the onboarding experience is broken down into four sections. This process is presented to the user when accessing the first launch where the user is prompted to enter their phone number.

Startup - Secret passphrase

The onboarding process then continues by prompting the user for a secret passphrase to identify them for future logins.

Startup - Add first contact

After entering the secret passphrase during the onboarding process, the user is prompted to create their first contact. This step involves adding the contact's name and phone number or email address to the user's account. Once the contact has been added, the user can easily initiate a call or message with them through the application.

Dashboard

The dashboard is designed to be user-friendly and uncluttered, with a simple layout that offers users a choice between two options: "Make a call" or "Settings". The buttons are designed to be easily recognizable through the use of corresponding icons, making it easy for users to associate each button with the intended action. In addition, the font used on the dashboard is large and the buttons are designed to be big, which makes it easier for elderly users or those with visual impairments to interact with the dashboard.

Contact Screen

The contact screen provides a convenient and organized way for users to view and manage their contacts. The side overview displays all of the user's contacts in a clear and concise manner. When the user selects a particular contact, the interface responds by displaying the contact's profile picture, name and number on the right side of the screen. This makes it easy for the user to quickly find and access the information they need, without having to search through a long list of contacts.

Add other contacts

This page allows the user to add new contacts by entering their name and phone number. Once the user enters this information, the contact will be added to their contact list.

Call Screen

This call screen is designed to make video conferencing easy and intuitive for the user. The left-hand side displays a list of all the user's contacts, allowing them to quickly add someone to the call. When the user selects a contact, they are prompted to either add the contact to the call or cancel the action. Once the call has started, the user has easy access to common actions like muting their microphone, turning on/off the camera, and ending the call with the corresponding buttons on the screen.

Account Settings

The account settings interface allows users to customize various aspects of their experience within the application. Firstly, the user has the ability to adjust the font size to better suit their visual needs. Additionally, they can select their preferred color scheme for the application. For those who prefer voice commands, the interface also offers a voice activation option. Users can adjust notification sounds to their desired volume level, choosing from quiet, regular, loud or vibrate. Lastly, the interface includes the option for users to delete their account or return to their dashboard.

Conclusion

Designing this application posed several challenges, with the most significant one being the need to understand the core problem at hand. We had to delve deeper into the underlying pain points that caused user frustrations due to the lack of elder-friendly design. Through careful analysis of the data gathered from our research, we were able to identify the most common problem areas faced by elder-users. In creating the web application, we made use of the insights gained during our research. Our goal was to design a web application that is not only easy to use but also simple and reliable.
